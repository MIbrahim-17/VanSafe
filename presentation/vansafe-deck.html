The problem

It started at my own front door.

Every school morning my parents faced the same anxiety — and so does every parent who sends a child off in a stranger's van.

  • Drivers go unresponsive — phones ring out, even mid-route, leaving parents with no idea where their child is.
  • Heavy traffic turns a 20-minute run into an hour; no live location to plan around it.
  • Extreme weather — 45°C+ heat and monsoon flooding make delays dangerous, not just inconvenient.
  • No way to verify trust — choosing a driver is word-of-mouth and hope, with no records, no reviews, no accountability.

VanSafe09
The flywheel

Why drivers opt in.

  • Credibility, not word-of-mouth — a verified profile & real reviews win parents a stranger never could.
  • More bookings — visibility in search & AI-match fills empty seats.
  • Money saved — route optimization cuts distance & fuel; a dashboard shows rupees saved each month.

Parents bring trust, drivers bring supply — and every trip quietly produces data.

VanSafe10
The real prize · Civic data

Every ride is an anonymised data point.

Ease school-hour congestion

The most-used school-run routes & corridors, mapped. Shared with traffic authorities to re-time signals and relieve the streets that choke at 7am & 1pm.

Faster emergency response

Route density & timing show where children move, when. Emergency services can plan infrastructure & cut response times on accident-prone corridors.

A consumer app on the surface; a public mobility dataset underneath.

VanSafe11
The real prize · Civic data

From rides to regulation & planning.

Fleet composition

Which vans & wagons dominate, and where — input for transport & emissions planning.

Safety hotspots

Where unverified & over-capacity vans operate — targeted child-safety regulation, not blanket crackdowns.

Fuel & emissions

Fuel consumed vs distance travelled — an economy & emissions signal optimization actively reduces.

Verification coverage, demand by neighbourhood, peak-hour pressure — all measurable for the first time.
